📊 Exhaustion-Failure-to-Continue Strategy

Visual Guide to Understanding the Mean Reversion Hypothesis

🎯 The Core Hypothesis

When markets show extreme directional movement but fail to continue, they tend to revert to the mean.

  1. EXHAUSTION: Market makes extreme move (wide range + consecutive bars + extreme close)
  2. FAILURE: Next bar closes back inside the previous bar's range (momentum dies)
  3. SIGNAL: Enter counter-trend position expecting mean reversion

🔑 Key Insights from Trade Analysis

Why Winners Win (65% of trades):

  • True exhaustion: Extreme moves with wide ranges (>1.5× median) run out of buyers/sellers
  • Failure confirms weakness: When price can't continue, it signals momentum shift
  • Mean reversion kicks in: Markets naturally pull back toward equilibrium

Why Losers Lose (35% of trades):

  • Trend too strong: Macroeconomic forces override technical exhaustion signals
  • False failures: Temporary pullbacks that look like failures but are just pauses
  • News events: Fundamental catalysts can overwhelm mean reversion tendency

Why 65% Win Rate is Excellent:
